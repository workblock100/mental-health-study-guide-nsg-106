ğŸ˜° Anxiety Disorders

A response to stress that becomes maladaptive when it interferes with functioning.

ğŸ“Š Levels

  • Mild: Sharp focus, good for learning.
  • Moderate: Narrowed field, needs redirection.
  • Severe: Doom, physical symptoms (headache).
  • Panic: Terror, hallucinations, unsafe.

ğŸ’Š Interventions

  • Mild/Mod: Active listening, open questions.
  • Severe/Panic: SAFETY. Stay with patient. Quiet room. Short/firm sentences.
  • Meds: Benzos (acute), SSRIs (long-term).

ğŸƒ Defense Mechanisms

  • Healthy: Altruism, Sublimation.
  • Intermediate: Repression, Displacement.
  • Immature: Projection, Splitting (Borderline).

ğŸ˜ Major Depressive Disorder

Persistent depressed mood > 2 weeks.

ğŸ’¡ Priority: Suicide Risk Assessment. Ask directly: "Do you have a plan?"

âš–ï¸ Legal & Ethical Issues

Balancing client rights with safety.

  • Voluntary Admission: Client agrees. Can request release (AMA), but provider can place hold if unsafe.
  • Involuntary Admission: Harm to self/others. Keeps right to refuse meds (unless court order).
  • Tarasoff: Duty to warn specific victims of threats.

ğŸš¨ Crisis Intervention

Acute, time-limited event (4-6 weeks).

  • Goal: Return to pre-crisis level of functioning.
  • Priority: Physical safety first.
  • Types: Maturational (marriage/retirement), Situational (job loss), Adventitious (disaster/crime).
